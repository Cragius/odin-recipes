Chili con Carne

A rich, meaty dish that, with a few simple additions, is fully staisfying

This is a great dish for a beginner cook. It cooks slowly, so you never feel hurried and never have to worry about precise timing or juggling different kitchen jobs concurrently. And of course it is also delicious.

Ingredients

  • 500g mince (turkey will do, but beef is best)
  • 1 400g can blaack beans
  • 1 400g can diced or peeled tomato
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 onion
  • 1 stick celery
  • 1 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1 tbsp chipotle chili in adobo sauce
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 2 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 tsp cayenne powder
  • salt to taste

    • To serve with it

  • Sprinkle of parmesan or other cheese
  • Handful of corn chips
  • Vinegary hot sauce (like Byron Bay Chili Co's Green Jalapeno sauce)

Steps

  1. Put pot on stove top on medium heat, add oil
  2. Dice (fairly finely) onion, carrot, celery, add to pot and cook for approx. 10 minutes
  3. Move vegetables aside and add tomato paste, allowing it to fry for half a minute before stirring in the vegetables
  4. Add mince and break it up with wooden spoon or masher
  5. Add spices
  6. AFte a few minutes, add can of tomato and chipotle chillies
  7. Cook until meat has lost its raw look, then add beans and 2 cups of water
  8. Turn heat up to bring to boil, then lower to a gentle simmer
  9. Periodically check to see if it is drying out or catching, if the former add a little more water, if the latter give it a quick stir
  10. After approximately one hour, taste to check seasoning
  11. If satiisfied, turn stive off and serve
  12. After adding the chili to your bowl, crush corn chips into it and stir through with the cheese and sauce
